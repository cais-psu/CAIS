Dr. Kovalenko, one of the faculty participants in the ICDS Digital Twins Hub’s Faculty-in-Residence Program, and Ph.D. candidate Hongliang Li were featured in a recent Penn State News story. The article highlights their team’s work on the Water Evaluation and Treatment Digital Twin (WET-DT) project.

The project aims to develop a digital twin framework for more robust and predictive process control in water treatment systems.

Read more at the [Digital Twins Hub’s Faculty-in-Residence Program highlights mentorship, research](https://www.psu.edu/news/institute-computational-and-data-sciences/story/digital-twins-hubs-faculty-residence-program){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}.
